# Problem
The user wants to build a decentralized online banking and fixed-term term deposit platform on Ethereum where depositors lock stablecoins to earn interest. Traditional ERC20 vault balances do not provide transferable or visually rich proof of deposit ownership.

# Reason
Users need a way to track, transfer, and redeem their locked savings positions, while the protocol needs a secure mechanism to manage interest pools separately from principal deposits.

# Solution
Design a dual-contract architecture:
1. `SavingCore`: An ERC721 NFT contract ("Saving Certificate" / `SCERT`) where each active deposit mints a unique NFT to the user.
2. `VaultManager`: A dedicated vault contract holding ERC20 token liquidity for paying out guaranteed APR interest upon deposit maturity.

# Decision
Selected Solidity `^0.8.28`, Hardhat, and OpenZeppelin v5 contracts (`ERC20`, `ERC721`, `Ownable`, `Pausable`, `ReentrancyGuard`). Fixed-term plans will support custom tenor (days), APR (BPS), min/max deposit limits, and early withdrawal penalties.
